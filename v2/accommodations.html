Where to stay

Renting out an empty apartment through Airbnb is a fantastic alternative to hotels in NYC. There are usually lots of great options available in Brooklyn in the Summer, often cheaper and more convenient than hotels. Some Brooklyn neighborhoods we recommend are Greenpoint, Williamsburg, Brooklyn Heights, Cobble Hill, Boerum Hill, Gowanus, Park Slope, Prospect Heights, and Fort Greene. Also check out Long Island City (right over the river in Queens).

If you have a larger group, VRBO (HomeAway) is another great option for renting out larger houses and apartments throughout the city. This may also be cheaper and more comfortable than a hotel.

If you’re looking for a hotel, here are a few suggestions:

NU Hotel
85 Smith St, Brooklyn, NY

NU Hotel is a great option if you’re planning on staying for a few days. It’s in a great location (we live a couple blocks away) and is close to many subway lines.

The Box House Hotel
77 Box St, Brooklyn, NY

If you’re in Brooklyn for a short amount of time, The Box House Hotel is very close to the wedding venue. It’s further from the subway, so plan on taking cabs a bit more to get around.

Wythe Hotel
80 Wythe Ave, Brooklyn, NY

A more upscale (and trendy) option is the Wythe Hotel. It’s located in Williamsburg, surrounded by lots of restaurants and shopping. Williamsburg is in between the wedding venue and the other weekend events.

Holiday Inn Express
625 Union St, Brooklyn, NY

A wallet-friendly option, located in an up-and-coming neighborhood that keeps getting more interesting, and short cab ride from both Friday and Sunday’s events. It’s also walking distance to some great bars and restaurants.
